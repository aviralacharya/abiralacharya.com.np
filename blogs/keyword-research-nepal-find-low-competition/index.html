The single biggest mistake I see Nepal businesses make with SEO is choosing the wrong keywords to target. They go after broad, high-volume terms — "hotel Nepal," "trekking company," "online shopping" — and wonder why six months of content effort produces zero ranking movement. The problem is never the content. It's the keyword strategy.

Keyword research is the process of identifying which search queries your target customers in Nepal are actually using, which of those queries have realistic ranking potential for your site, and — most critically — which ones lead to actual conversions, not just traffic. Done correctly, a solid keyword research strategy produces a semantic content cluster: a structured set of pages that cover a topic so comprehensively that Google recognises your site as the topical authority in your niche.

In 2026, that topical authority signal has become more important than ever — not just for traditional Google rankings, but for being cited in Google's AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity, where the sites that answer the most questions about a topic most clearly get cited most often.

This guide covers the complete keyword research framework I use for Nepal businesses: the best free keyword research tools, how to find low-competition keywords that convert, and how to organise those keywords into semantic content clusters that build lasting topical authority.

Why Keyword Research Is Different in Nepal

Nepal's search landscape has a specific set of characteristics that make generic global keyword research advice partially inapplicable. Understanding these Nepal-specific factors is the first step to finding keywords that actually work:

  • Search volumes are lower — but so is competition. A keyword getting 500 monthly searches in Nepal might represent an accessible #1 ranking opportunity, while the same volume in the US would be laughably easy. Nepal's lower volume comes with proportionally lower competition, which means your ROI on targeting "easier" keywords is often far higher than global benchmarks suggest.
  • Bilingual search patterns. Nepal's search queries happen in both English and Nepali. Most business-intent searches (services, products, bookings) happen in English, while many informational and question-based searches happen in Nepali script or Romanised Nepali. A complete keyword strategy covers both.
  • City-level local intent is underserved. "Salon Kathmandu," "trekking Pokhara," "hospital Biratnagar" — city-qualified keywords in Nepal are dramatically underserved by optimised content. Most Nepal business websites target only the generic term, leaving the specific city-qualified variant — which carries much higher commercial intent — completely uncontested.
  • Seasonal patterns are pronounced. Nepal tourism keywords spike sharply in October–November (autumn trekking season) and March–April (spring season). Educational keywords peak around admissions periods (May–July). Keyword research in Nepal must account for these cycles.
  • Mobile-first search. 70–80% of searches in Nepal happen on mobile. This changes which keyword formats perform well — shorter queries, voice-search-style questions, and location-qualified terms all perform above the global average.

Understanding Keyword Types: Informational, Commercial, Transactional

Before you open any keyword research tool, you need to understand the three fundamental keyword intents — because targeting the wrong intent for your page type is as harmful as targeting the wrong keyword entirely. Every keyword a Nepal user types into Google falls into one of three categories:

I

Informational Keywords CONTENT PAGES

The user wants to learn something. No immediate purchase intent. Best for blog posts, guides, FAQs.

Informational keywords make up the majority of searches in Nepal's market. They are questions, how-to queries, and educational searches. These keywords build your topical authority and bring top-of-funnel visitors — people who don't know you yet.

Nepal examples: "what is the best trekking season Nepal," "how to start a business in Nepal," "BCA subjects Tribhuvan University," "how to rank website on Google Nepal," "best places to visit Pokhara."

Use for: Blog posts, guides, FAQ pages, pillar content, educational articles. These pages do not need a hard sell — they need to answer the question completely and funnel readers toward your commercial pages via internal links.

C

Commercial Investigation Keywords REVIEW & COMPARE PAGES

The user is comparing options before buying. High intent, moderate competition. Best for comparison and review pages.

Commercial investigation keywords indicate a user who is actively evaluating options — comparing prices, reading reviews, or shortlisting service providers. In Nepal, these keywords often include "best," "top," "vs," "review," "price," or "cost."

Nepal examples: "best trekking companies Nepal 2026," "SEMrush vs Ahrefs for Nepal SEO," "hotel Kathmandu price comparison," "BCA vs BIT which is better Nepal," "SEO consultant Nepal rates."

Use for: Comparison pages, listicles ("top 10"), review articles, "best X in Nepal" content. These pages should be detailed, factual, and include your own service or product as a credible option.

T

Transactional Keywords MONEY PAGES

The user is ready to act right now. Highest commercial value. Best for service pages, product pages, booking pages.

Transactional keywords signal immediate purchase or action intent. They often include words like "buy," "hire," "book," "order," "near me," "contact," or a specific service/product name with a location. These are your highest-value keywords — the ones that directly drive revenue.

Nepal examples: "SEO consultant Kathmandu hire," "trek booking Pokhara," "web design company Nepal contact," "buy laptop Kathmandu online," "salon near me Lalitpur," "BCA admission form 2026 Mechi Campus."

Use for: Homepage, service pages, product pages, contact pages, booking pages. These pages need clear calls-to-action, trust signals (reviews, credentials), and fast load times — not long editorial content.

Google Keyword Planner — Your Free Nepal Volume Source

1

Google Keyword Planner 100% FREE

ads.google.com/keywordplanner — The only free keyword research tool that provides real search volume data sourced directly from Google, filterable to Nepal.

Google Keyword Planner is the foundational free keyword research tool that every Nepal SEO professional must master. It is built into Google Ads — you need a free Google Ads account to access it, but you do not need to spend any money on ads. The data comes directly from Google's search index, making it the most accurate volume data available for Nepal.

The key limitation: Without an active ad campaign, Google shows volume in ranges ("100–1,000" rather than "480"). For most Nepal keyword decisions, this range is still sufficient — you're distinguishing between keywords with 100 searches vs 10,000 searches, and the ranges capture that difference clearly.

How I use Google Keyword Planner for Nepal SEO:

  • Set location to Nepal — always filter by Nepal before pulling data. The default global data is meaningless for Nepal-specific strategy. Go to Settings → Location → add Nepal.
  • "Discover new keywords" — enter a seed term like "trekking Nepal" and Google generates hundreds of related keyword ideas with Nepal-specific volume ranges. This is my primary source for initial keyword ideation for new Nepal client niches.
  • "Get search volume and forecasts" — paste a list of keywords you've already identified (from competitor research, GSC, customer interviews) and get volume data for all of them in bulk. I do this to validate keywords before assigning them to pages.
  • Competition column — this shows advertiser competition, not organic SEO competition. But high advertiser competition strongly correlates with commercial keyword intent — keywords that advertisers pay for are keywords that convert. I flag any Nepal keyword with "High" advertiser competition as a priority commercial target.
  • Top of page bid (high range) — what advertisers pay per click for this keyword in Nepal. A high bid indicates commercial intent. A "salon Kathmandu" keyword with a Rs. 80+ bid is far more commercially valuable than an informational keyword with a Rs. 5 bid.

Nepal-specific workflow: I open Keyword Planner for every new Nepal client niche, enter 5–10 seed terms their business relates to, filter by Nepal, download the full results as a CSV, then sort by estimated monthly searches (descending) and competition (low first). This produces the initial long list of candidate keywords — typically 300–800 keywords — that I then filter down to the priority targets.

How to Find Low-Competition Keywords That Actually Convert

Finding low-competition keywords for Nepal isn't just about finding keywords with low difficulty scores. A keyword can be easy to rank for but completely useless for conversion. The goal is finding the intersection of three factors: low difficulty + meaningful volume + conversion intent. Here is the exact process I follow:

①

Step 1 — Build Your Initial Long List GOOGLE KEYWORD PLANNER

Open Google Keyword Planner, set location to Nepal, and enter 5–10 seed keywords related to your business. Download all results as a CSV. You should end up with 300–1,000 keyword ideas. This is your raw material — do not edit yet, just collect.

Good seed keywords for Nepal: your core service + "Nepal," your core service + main city, your product category, your competitors' brand names (to find searchers evaluating your space), common questions your customers ask you in person.

②

Step 2 — Filter by Volume Floor and Difficulty Ceiling AHREFS + SEMRUSH

Apply two filters to your long list. First, set a minimum volume floor — I typically use 50 monthly searches in Nepal as the minimum. Below this, rankings rarely drive meaningful traffic even if you hit #1. Second, set a maximum difficulty ceiling — for new Nepal sites (Domain Rating under 20), target keywords with Ahrefs KD under 20. For established Nepal sites (DR 20–40), you can target up to KD 35.

Run your shortlisted keywords through Ahrefs free keyword generator to get KD scores, then cross-reference with SEMrush keyword difficulty using your daily free searches. Keywords where both tools agree on "low" difficulty are your safest targets.

③

Step 3 — SERP Analysis: Who Is Actually Ranking? MANUAL GOOGLE CHECK

For every priority keyword, open an incognito Chrome window (to avoid personalised results) and search the keyword in Google.com.np. Study the top-5 results:

  • Are the top results from large authority sites? If Wikipedia, TripAdvisor, Lonely Planet, or major news sites dominate the top 3, it will be very hard for a Nepal SMB to dislodge them — even with great content.
  • Are the top results poorly optimised? Generic pages, no clear target keyword in title tags, thin content — this is a clear signal that a well-optimised Nepal page can outrank them.
  • Are local Nepal sites present? If .com.np domains or known Nepal blogs rank in the top 5, that confirms the SERP is accessible to Nepal sites with reasonable authority.
  • What is the content quality like? If you can create a page that is clearly more comprehensive, more up-to-date, and more useful than the top results, you have a realistic path to ranking.
④

Step 4 — Prioritise by Conversion Intent Score MANUAL SCORING

Not all low-competition keywords are equally valuable. I assign each keyword a conversion intent score from 1–5 based on how likely a search is to lead to a business action:

  • Score 5 (Transactional): "hire SEO consultant Nepal," "book trekking Pokhara," "web design company Kathmandu contact" → target immediately, these drive revenue
  • Score 4 (Commercial): "best SEO company Nepal 2026," "top trekking agencies Kathmandu," "affordable web design Nepal" → high priority, close to purchase
  • Score 3 (Mixed intent): "SEO tools Nepal," "trekking packages Nepal price" → medium priority, good for blog content that funnels to service pages
  • Score 2 (Informational, relevant): "how to do SEO Nepal," "trekking permits Nepal" → lower priority, but builds topical authority and feeds the content cluster
  • Score 1 (Informational, tangential): "what is SEO," "Nepal mountains" → lowest priority, only pursue to fill out a comprehensive content cluster

Sort your final keyword list by Conversion Intent Score (descending), then by Volume (descending). Your top-20 keywords by this sort are your immediate priority targets.

Building a Semantic Content Cluster for Nepal Topical Authority

Finding individual keywords is only step one. The most powerful Nepal SEO strategy in 2026 is organising those keywords into semantic content clusters — a structured web of interlinked pages that together cover a topic so comprehensively that Google recognises your site as the authority on that topic. This is how you build topical authority.

🌐 What Is a Semantic Content Cluster?

A semantic content cluster consists of three types of pages working together:

  1. 1. Pillar Page: A comprehensive, authoritative page targeting a broad, high-volume keyword. This is the "hub" of your cluster. Example: "Trekking in Nepal — The Complete Guide" targeting "trekking Nepal."
  2. 2. Cluster / Support Pages: Individual pages each targeting a specific sub-topic keyword related to the pillar. Each page covers one topic deeply and links back to the pillar. Examples: "Everest Base Camp Trek Cost 2026," "Best Trekking Season Nepal," "Annapurna Circuit Permit Guide."
  3. 3. Internal Linking Structure: Every cluster page links to the pillar page using keyword-rich anchor text. The pillar page links back to all cluster pages. This creates a clear topical architecture that Google's crawlers and ranking algorithms understand and reward.

Why this works: When Google sees one site with 15 pages all deeply covering different aspects of "trekking Nepal" and all cross-linking to each other, it recognises that site as a topical authority on Nepal trekking. This authority lifts rankings for all pages in the cluster — not just the individual pages. Single standalone pages cannot compete with a well-structured cluster for competitive Nepal keywords.

How to build your semantic cluster map using free tools:

  • Google Keyword Planner → "Discover keywords" from your pillar keyword — the tool groups related keywords into themes automatically. Each theme = a potential cluster page topic.

  • Ahrefs free keyword generator → "Questions" tab — filters keyword ideas to only question-format queries. These are ideal cluster page topics because they map directly to FAQ schema and AI Overview citation opportunities.

Keyword Mapping — Assigning Keywords to Pages

Once you have your keyword cluster built, the final step is keyword mapping — the process of assigning each keyword to a specific page on your site (or a planned new page) and ensuring no two pages target the same keyword. Keyword cannibalisation — two pages on the same Nepal site competing for the same keyword — is one of the most common and damaging SEO mistakes I find in Nepal site audits.

✦

claude.ai — How I use Claude to turn raw keyword lists into structured content cluster maps and keyword assignment documents.

Claude AI has become an essential part of my keyword research workflow — not for generating keyword data (the tools above do that), but for organising and structuring keyword data into actionable cluster plans. Here's exactly how:

  • Cluster mapping from keyword list: I paste a raw list of 50–200 keywords from Google Keyword Planner into Claude and ask it to group them into semantic clusters, identify the best pillar keyword for each cluster, and suggest which keywords map to informational vs. commercial pages. This takes 30 minutes manually and 2 minutes with Claude.
  • Keyword cannibalisation audit: I provide Claude with a list of existing Nepal client pages and their target keywords, and ask it to identify any pages that are likely cannibalising each other. It flags conflicts far faster than any manual review.
  • Search intent classification: Paste 50 keywords and ask Claude to classify each as informational, commercial, or transactional based on the query structure. This saves hours of manual intent analysis.
  • Content brief generation: For each cluster page, I ask Claude to generate a detailed content brief — recommended H2 structure, semantic keywords to include, key questions to answer, schema types to add, and internal linking recommendations. A brief that would take 45 minutes to write manually takes 5 minutes with Claude.
  • Competitor gap analysis summary: I paste the top-ranking content for a Nepal keyword and ask Claude to identify what topics, questions, and angles the content doesn't cover — revealing the gaps my Nepal client can fill to outrank the existing result.

What is a semantic content cluster and why does it matter for Nepal SEO?

For Nepal SEO, clusters matter because Google's algorithms reward topical authority — sites that comprehensively cover a topic outrank sites with isolated individual pages, even if the individual pages are well-written. Building clusters is the most reliable path to sustainable Nepal rankings in 2026, especially as AI-powered search increasingly rewards authoritative sources.
